# Bikes

Notes on the bikes in the quiver and what each is suited for.

## Trek Roscoe 8

Roughly $1,600 hardtail and the best of the entry-level aluminum range, bought for riding fun trails. The Roscoe is a trail-oriented hardtail with slack geometry and wide plus tires, so it suits chunky and technical singletrack rather than fast end-of-pack Tour Divide racing.

## Wishlist: carbon XC race bike

Around the $6,000 mark, the pick is the Trek Supercaliber SL 9.7 GX AXS Gen 2 at $6,499. It is the cheapest Supercaliber with electronic shifting, since the next step down (SL 9.6 at $4,799) is mechanical Deore/SLX. It uses an SL OCLV Mountain Carbon frame, IsoStrut suspension (110mm RockShox SID fork, 80mm rear), SRAM GX AXS wireless 1x12, and Bontrager Kovee Comp 25 wheels, at roughly 12.2 kg. The SLR layup builds (9.8 and up) start around $9,000, and the Procaliber Gen 3 hardtail tops out near $2,500 in the US. Confirm 2026 pricing and availability with a local dealer.
